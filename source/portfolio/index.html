What drives my work?

Defining the right problem is the hard work.

Process

My process is a mix of service design thinking, lean UX, and being comfortable with the messiness of designing products and services.

Process

It might theoretically look like this.

  • Understand the goal
  • Frame the problem
  • Ideate and explore
  • Refine concepts
  • Build something
  • Validate what you've built
  • Measure to see if it meets your goals

Research

You can't do design without research. That includes competitive, market, and user research. They all play a part in defining the aspects of a design.

Qualitative

  • Why is there a problem?
  • What is the need?
  • Who has the problem?

Quantitative

  • Where are there problems?
  • Understand aggregate behaviour
  • Split test and compare
  • How does work impact business goals?

Participatory

Involve everyone in the design process. That means designers, developers, product owners, and especially customers and end users.

Mobile Insights

The Problem: Identifying the needs of business end users for mobile analytics tools and then designing a sytem to support those use cases.

Overview

I worked with a small team of designers to help understand the needs of business end users around the questions they ask of their data. We then designed a mobile design system that would allow the existing analytics platform to be useful on a mobile device.

Hypothesis and Qs

  • The needs of a business end user are significantly more constrained than those of a business analyst.
  • The mobile form factor requires significant constraints when exploring large and complex data sets.
  • How deep to business users want to go within a data set before moving to more complex tools?
  • What are the key questions that business users ask of their data?

SMB Business Tools

The Problem: What are the needs of high-growth small and medium businesses that are unmet with the existing suite of CRM and communication tools?

Overview

I worked with a small team of designers on a very exploratory research and design project focused around the unmet needs of high-growth SMB companies.

Hypothesis and Qs

  • The way new businesses are working is significantly different from the enterprises of old.
  • The tools of old enterprises are siloed to specific roles whereas many new businesses have different ways of viewing organizational structure.

Stacks

The Problem: Users are heavily multitasking in Salesforce and don't have an efficient way of organizing their various distinct workflows independent from each other.

Overview

We created the concept of stacks of works, similar to organizing ones work on paper in various folders or discrete chunks that are separately accessible. This allows users to maintain context within a workflow.

Hypothesis and Qs

  • Stacks usage drives the sales process forward, rather than just about activity accounting.
  • Stacks are a better way of supporting the kind of multitasking that salespeople have to do.
  • What kind of work is required to guess when a stack expires?
  • How many workstreams does a person handle at a time?
